METHODOLOGY
================================================================================

This assessment employs a HYBRID approach combining two research-backed methods:

1. REFERENCE CLASSIFICATION (Percentile-Based):
   Following Waltman & Schreiber (2013), individual references are classified
   using percentile ranks, comparing each reference to others within the thesis.

   Thresholds (adaptive to your thesis):
     • Excellent: Top 25% (≥0.047)
     • Good: 50-75th percentile (0.030-0.047)
     • Fair: 25-50th percentile (0.004-0.030)
     • Poor: Bottom 25% (<0.004)

2. THESIS CLASSIFICATION (Absolute Standards):
   Following Hicks et al. (2015) and Costas et al. (2013), overall thesis
   quality is assessed against absolute minimum standards, avoiding the
   circular logic of self-referential percentiles.

   Thresholds (field-normalized for systematic reviews):
     • High Quality (A): ≥0.35 (Huang 2008)
     • Medium Quality (B): ≥0.20 (Gomaa & Fahmy 2013)
     • Low Quality (C): <0.20

Rationale for Hybrid Approach:
  The Leiden Manifesto (Hicks et al., 2015) recommends using multiple
  indicators for different purposes. Percentile-based classification is
  appropriate for comparing publications WITHIN a field (Waltman & Schreiber,
  2013), while absolute thresholds are needed when assessing against minimum
  standards (Wilsdon et al., 2015). Using percentiles for thesis-level
  classification would create invalid self-referential comparison (Costas
  et al., 2013).
